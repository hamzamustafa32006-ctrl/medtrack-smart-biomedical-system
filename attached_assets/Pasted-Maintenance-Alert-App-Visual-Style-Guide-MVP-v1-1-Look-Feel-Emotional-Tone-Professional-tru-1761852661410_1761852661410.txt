Maintenance Alert App – Visual Style Guide (MVP v1)
1. Look & Feel
Emotional Tone:
Professional, trustworthy, and calm.
Clear and focused: emphasizes reliability and precision.
Minimalist: avoids clutter, highlights actionable items.
Brand Personality (as expressed through UI):
Friendly but authoritative: guides engineers confidently without being overwhelming.
Efficient & practical: prioritizes essential functions like alerts and history tracking.
Calm & reassuring: communicates reliability, critical for hospital/industrial environments.
2. Color Palette
Role	Color	HEX	RGB	Usage
Primary	Blue	#0057B7	(0, 87, 183)	Main navigation, primary buttons, top bar, active states.
Secondary 1	Dark Gray	#333333	(51, 51, 51)	Body text, subheadings, inactive items.
Secondary 2	Light Gray	#F4F4F4	(244, 244, 244)	Backgrounds, cards, containers, secondary sections.
Accent	Orange	#FF6D00	(255, 109, 0)	Alerts, notifications, CTAs requiring attention (e.g., overdue contracts).
Usage Notes:
Use primary blue for actionable elements like buttons and highlighted active states.
Secondary grays provide hierarchy and reduce visual noise.
Accent orange should be used sparingly for urgent alerts or important highlights.
Backgrounds remain light and neutral to maximize readability.
3. Typography
Role	Font	Weight	Size	Usage / Notes
Headings	Inter / Roboto	Bold (700)	H1: 32px, H2: 24px, H3: 18px	Clean, modern, professional tone.
Body Text	Inter / Roboto	Regular (400)	Body: 16px, Small: 14px	Legible, practical, for lists, descriptions, and card content.
Captions / Labels	Inter / Roboto	Medium (500)	12px	Subtle info like contract dates or metadata.
Typography Tone:
Modern, clean, professional.
Focus on legibility in mobile and tablet contexts.
Minimal use of decorative fonts to maintain seriousness.
4. UI Elements
Buttons:
Shape: Rectangular with rounded corners (8px radius).
Primary button: Blue background (#0057B7), white text.
Secondary button: Light Gray (#F4F4F4) background, Dark Gray (#333333) text.
Hover / Active: Slight shadow or darker blue (#004499).
Disabled: Gray (#CCCCCC) background, light gray text.
Input Fields:
Style: Rectangular with 6px border-radius.
Border: Light gray (#CCCCCC), turns primary blue (#0057B7) on focus.
Labels: Above input, regular 14px font, dark gray (#333333).
Placeholder text: Light gray (#AAAAAA).
Navigation:
Mobile: Bottom tab bar, icons + labels.
Top bar: Page title, optional search icon.
Desktop/Tablet: Top bar with dropdown menu or sidebar for additional options.
Cards / Containers:
Background: White (#FFFFFF) or light gray (#F4F4F4).
Rounded corners: 8px.
Shadow: Subtle (#000000, 5% opacity) to lift content.
Padding: 16px standard.
Used for maintenance history, contract details, or alerts.
5. Iconography & Imagery
Icons:
Style: Minimalist, outline icons.
Size: 24px standard, 16px for inline actions.
Use: Represent equipment, alerts, notifications, history, and settings.
Images / Illustrations:
Prefer abstract or schematic illustrations for onboarding/tutorial screens.
Avoid realistic or stock photos of people.
Maintain clean, flat, minimal design to reduce visual distraction.
6. Layout & Spacing
Grid System:
8px base spacing (margins, paddings, gutters).
Consistent alignment of cards, inputs, and buttons.
White Space:
Use generously to reduce cognitive load.
Ensure clear separation between alerts, lists, and contract details.
Screen Ratios / Responsive Design:
Mobile: 360–414px width (standard smartphones).
Tablet: 768px width.
Desktop: Optional for admin view, 1024px+ width.
Flexible columns: 1 column on mobile, 2–3 on tablet/desktop.
7. Brand Consistency Notes
DOs:
Keep a consistent blue primary theme for key actions.
Use orange accent only for urgent alerts.
Maintain clean typography for readability.
Use consistent spacing and alignment across screens.
Highlight actionable items clearly; avoid overloading the user with info.
DON’Ts:
Don’t use multiple accent colors that dilute the alert system.
Don’t use decorative fonts or heavy textures.
Don’t crowd screens with too much text or buttons.
Don’t use realistic photos that conflict with the professional tone.
Alignment with Product Mission:
The design communicates trust, reliability, and efficiency.
Minimalist approach ensures users focus on critical maintenance alerts and history.
Supports quick adoption and reduces cognitive load for engineers in high-stakes environments.
